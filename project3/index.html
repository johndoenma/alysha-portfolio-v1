ABOUT HELSINKI

Helsinki is the capital and largest city of Finland, close to Estonia and Russia. It is a vibrant city known for its clean environment, and unique blend of Nordic and Eastern European influences.

Tourists are drawn to its scenic archipelago, historic sites, modern museums, lively food scene, and year-round cultural events. Despite being a capital, Helsinki maintains a relaxed, small-city atmosphere, with a strong focus on sustainability, smart city development, and nature. Helsinki combines history, innovation, and natural beauty, making it one of the most livable and forward-thinking cities in Europe.

HOW IT CAME TO BE

My main inspiration for the design is the strong influence of Art Nouveau that characterizes much of Helsinkiâ€™s architecture. As I delved into this style, I focused on researching the distinctive architectural elements that define it. One feature that stood out to me was the windows that are crafted with unique arches and intricate organic shapes integrated within the frames.

These nature-inspired forms give the buildings a sense of elegance that reflects the Art Nouveau ethos of blending natural beauty with functional design. This exploration of window design has greatly influenced the aesthetic direction of my project, encouraging me to incorporate similar curves and motifs that celebrate both form and artistry.
